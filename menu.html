Nutrition Information

People with diabetes should focus on foods that are low in refined carbohydrates and added sugars. Good options include:

  • Non-starchy vegetables like leafy greens, broccoli, and peppers
  • Lean proteins such as chicken, fish, and tofu
  • Healthy fats from sources like avocados, nuts, and olive oil
  • Whole grains in moderate portions

For those with hypertension or heart concerns, consider these guidelines:

  • Choose foods low in sodium and saturated fat
  • Include sources of omega-3 fatty acids like salmon and walnuts
  • Incorporate potassium-rich foods like bananas and sweet potatoes
  • Opt for lean proteins and plant-based options when possible

For those with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity, safe options include:

  • Naturally gluten-free grains like rice and quinoa
  • Fresh fruits and vegetables
  • Unprocessed meats, fish, and poultry
  • Legumes, nuts, and seeds
